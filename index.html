üå± ORGANIC - WHEN IT MATTERS

Priority for Organic (If Budget Allows):

  • Berries (strawberries, blueberries - heavy pesticides)
  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Apples
  • Eggs (or pasture-raised minimum)
  • Dairy (grass-fed > organic > conventional)

Don't Stress About Organic:

  • Avocados (thick skin protects)
  • Bananas (same)
  • Potatoes (conventional fine)
  • Rice, oats (minimal concern)

The Rule:

Organic when you can, especially for daily large quantities. But:

Conventional whole foods > Organic processed foods > Conventional processed foods

üéØ The Reality Check on Food Quality

Perfect is the enemy of good.

  • Grass-fed steak everyday? Not realistic for most budgets
  • Wild salmon every meal? Nope
  • All organic everything? Probably not happening

The Strategy:

  • Get premium stuff when you can (one good meal/day)
  • Wild game when available (zero cost if you hunt)
  • Regular grocery meat still good (way better than processed)
  • Focus on WHOLE FOODS first, quality second
  • Listen to your body's response

80% whole foods, home-cooked = WINS | 20% convenience = not gonna kill you

But flip that ratio and you'll feel and perform like shit.
